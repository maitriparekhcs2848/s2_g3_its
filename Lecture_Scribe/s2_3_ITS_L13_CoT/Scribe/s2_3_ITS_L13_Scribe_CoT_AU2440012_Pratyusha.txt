Q1. Partitioning 20 Dishes into 4 Platters

Problem Structure

\vspace{1em}

We are given:

\vspace{1em}

20 distinct dishes

\vspace{0.5em}

Divided into 4 groups

\vspace{0.5em}

Each group has exactly 5 dishes

\vspace{0.5em}

Order within each group does not matter

\vspace{0.5em}

Order of groups does not matter

\vspace{1.5em}

(a) Total Number of Ways

\vspace{1em}

Since:

\vspace{1em}

There are 20 distinct objects.

\vspace{0.5em}

We divide them into 4 unlabeled groups.

\vspace{0.5em}

Each group contains 5 elements.

\vspace{0.5em}

This is a multinomial counting problem.

\vspace{1em}

If the groups were ordered, the number of ways would be:

\vspace{1em}

\[
\frac{20!}{(5!)^4}
\]

\vspace{2em}

However, the groups themselves are unlabeled. Therefore, we divide by $4!$ to remove overcounting due to rearrangements of the groups.

\vspace{1em}

Hence, total number of ways:

\vspace{1em}

\[
\frac{20!}{(5!)^4 4!}
\]

\vspace{2em}

(b) Probability That Each Platter Has One Cuisine

\vspace{1em}

Given:

\vspace{1em}

4 cuisines

\vspace{0.5em}

Each cuisine has exactly 5 dishes

\vspace{1em}

Thus:

\vspace{1em}

Each cuisine must form exactly one platter.

\vspace{1em}

Since platters are unlabeled, this arrangement is unique.

\vspace{1em}

Total favorable arrangements = 1

\vspace{1em}

Total possible arrangements =

\vspace{1em}

\[
\frac{20!}{(5!)^4 4!}
\]

\vspace{2em}

Therefore:

\vspace{1em}

\[
P = \frac{1}{\frac{20!}{(5!)^4 4!}}
= \frac{(5!)^4 4!}{20!}
\]

\vspace{2em}

From the solution slide:

\vspace{1em}

\[
P = \frac{(5!)^4}{20!}
\]

\noindent
Q2. Bus Waiting Time (Uniform Distribution)

\vspace{1em}

Given:

\vspace{1em}

Buses every 15 minutes.

\vspace{0.5em}

Passenger arrives uniformly between 7:00–7:30.

\vspace{0.5em}

Total interval length = 30 minutes.

\vspace{1em}

Uniform distribution implies:

\vspace{1em}

\[
P = \frac{\text{favorable time}}{\text{total time}}
\]

\vspace{2em}

(a) Wait Less Than 5 Minutes

\vspace{1em}

Passenger waits less than 5 minutes if arrival occurs in:

\vspace{1em}

7:10–7:15

\vspace{0.5em}

7:25–7:30

\vspace{1em}

Each interval = 5 minutes

\vspace{1em}

Total favorable time = 10 minutes

\vspace{1em}

\[
P = \frac{10}{30}
= \frac{1}{3}
\]

\vspace{2em}

(b) Wait More Than 10 Minutes

\vspace{1em}

Occurs if arrival is:

\vspace{1em}

7:00–7:05

\vspace{0.5em}

7:15–7:20

\vspace{1em}

Total favorable time = 10 minutes

\vspace{1em}

\[
P = \frac{10}{30}
= \frac{1}{3}
\]
